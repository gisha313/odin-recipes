Sarma (Stuffed Cabbage)

Home

Description

For this satisfying Sarma dish, sour cabbage (sauerkraut) leaves are stuffed with a seasoned mixture of rice and ground meat (usually pork). They are then sandwiched between two layers of sauerkraut and simmered to completion. This is the best stuffed sour cabbage recipe I've ever tried.

Ingredients (for 8 servings)

  • 1 large head sour cabbage
  • 2 pounds ground pork
  • 1 cup uncooked white rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 cup tomato juice
  • 1 cup water, or as needed to cover
  • 2 tablespoon oil

Steps

  1. Remove the leaves from the head of sour cabbage.
  2. Fry diced onion on a pan, and then add to it ground pork, washed white rice, salt and pepper.
  3. Wrap about a 1/2 cup of mixure with a sour cabbage leaf at a time.
  4. Cover the bottom of a large pot with sour cabbage leaves.
  5. Layer cabbage rolls on top, placing them seam-side down.
  6. Pour tomato juice over rolls, and then add enough water to cover.
  7. Cover the rolls with a layer of sour cabbage leaves on top.
  8. Bring the rolls to a boil, reduce the heat to low and let simmer for at least 6 hours, or overnight.
